Random Illumination Microscopy

It combines the resolution of the best SIMs with the ease of use and applicability of the standard fluoresence microscope. Unlike conventional super-resolution methods, RIM is based on random illumination of the sample and thus overcomes the complexity associated with controlling the illumination function.

Moreover, a statistical approach of this random illumination shows that it is insensitive to aberrations. Consequently, RIM operates under conditions inaccessible to SIM, in particular on living biological tissue.

RIM

RIM achieves a super-resolution level comparable with the best interference-based 3D SIM. The gain of resolution in three dimensions was obtained by using a novel data-processing method combining the statistical approach of fluctuation microscopy with the demodulation principle of SIM.
